FONTLOG for the Beeraw Hex font family
======================================

This file provides detailed information on the Beeraw Hex font family.
This information should be distributed along with the Beeraw Hex fonts and
any derivative works.


Basic font information
----------------------

Beeraw Hex is a geometric, strictly monolinear (90-unit stroke) display
sans derived from the hexagonal "alveole" cells of the beeraw honey brand
logo. Flat-topped / flat-bottomed hexagonal cells, vertical side edges,
bevelled and rounded corners.

The family spans two axes: weight (stroke 40 / 64 / 90 / 130 units) and width
(normal / Wide, x1.35), for eight masters. Neither axis touches the
perpendicular stroke, which stays at its master's nominal value everywhere --
that monoline is the DNA of the design.

There are two build paths. The base Regular ships normalised and is built from
the UFO sources/BeerawHex-Regular.ufo (+ features.fea); the other seven masters
come straight from the parametric generator font_build.py. The files in fonts/
(TTF, OTF, WOFF2, WOFF) are build artifacts, regenerated from the source and
never edited by hand. See README.md for the build steps.


ChangeLog
---------

2.008  (2026-07)
- Four new masters, completing a 4 x 2 matrix (eight masters): UltraLight (100,
  stroke 40) and Light (300, stroke 64), at both the normal and the Wide width.
  UltraLight and Light carry typographic names (nameID 16/17) so the four
  weights regroup under one family in modern apps while nameID 1/2 stay
  legacy-compatible.
- The corner-rounding radius now tracks the stroke (capped at its former 26 u).
  round_corners() opens the shape with that radius, which erases ink thinner
  than twice it -- a fixed 26 annihilated a light master outright (measured: at
  stroke 45 the glyph came out with zero area). Regular and every heavier master
  are bit-identical to before.
- Glyph corrections, all from constants that did not track the stroke:
    * j and g -- the stem started at a hard-coded offset that only equalled the
      stroke at Regular, so the descender hook came away from the stem entirely
      at the light weights (a 50 u gap at UltraLight).
    * 8 -- the two bowls had a fixed geometry, so the waist was never exactly one
      stroke wide: 96 u at Regular, 176 u at Bold, and at UltraLight the walls no
      longer met at all (two strokes with a gap, reading as one heavy band). The
      bowls are now sized from the waist outwards, so it is exactly W everywhere.
    * guillemets -- the two chevrons sat close enough to be welded into a solid
      blob at Bold weight. tools/redraw_glyphs.py already carried this fix for
      the normalised Regular; it is back-ported to the generator so every master
      gets it.
    * the traced arobase tore in two under the erosion used to bring it down to
      the UltraLight stroke; the erosion is backed off to just short of tearing.
- QA: the monoline gate now measures at two pixels per unit. At one pixel per
  unit the distance transform quantises widths to ~1 u steps -- harmless at 90 u
  but 2.5 % at the UltraLight's 40 u, where it reported a spurious 42.0 and
  failed a master whose geometry measures a clean 40.0.
- The specimen embeds all eight masters as WOFF2 and is generated from the
  MASTERS table, so it no longer goes stale when a master is added. Eight
  masters now weigh less than four did as TTF (~119 KB against ~170 KB).

2.007  (2026-07)
- Three new masters, taking the family from one style to four across two axes:
  Beeraw Hex Bold, plus the wider family Beeraw Hex Wide (Regular and Bold).
  Each width is its own RIBBI family (usWidthClass 5 / 7).
- Weight axis: the Bold is simply a heavier monoline -- same skeleton, same
  advances, same kerning, stroke 90 -> 130 units.
- Width axis: the Wide masters keep the stroke and grow the round half-widths
  and the spacing by 1.35. Because every stem is drawn as a fixed-width bar,
  widening cannot thicken it: the monoline gate holds by construction.
- The parametric generator gained a MASTERS table (stroke x width_factor) as the
  single place both axes are defined.
- Glyph corrections, all fixed at the source and therefore across every master.
  Each came from a constant that did not track the stroke or the width:
    * A -- the crossbar overshot the legs (its x-range was fixed while the legs
      move inward as the letter widens); it is now trimmed to the legs' own
      silhouette, so it lands flush at any width or weight.
    * U -- nubs protruded into the counter, because the ring was clipped at the
      OUTER shoulder while the inner bevel starts lower, and lower still as the
      stroke thickens. Rebuilt with straight sides to the top.
    * Q -- the tail started at the centre of the bowl and cut across the counter
      while barely emerging outside. It is now trimmed at both ends: minus the
      counter, and bounded to a constant protrusion past the outer edge.
    * s -- mismatched, stair-stepped terminals in bold: the bowl kept a pure
      proportion, so the wall ate the counter down to 35 u, and the aperture box
      was aligned on a ratio that only matched the Regular by coincidence.
    * c and e -- protruding, uneven terminals in bold: a fixed aperture height
      overflowed the counter once the wall thickened and bit into the ring's
      arcs. Both are now bounded by the counter AND the straight-wall span.
    * C -- widened 6 % against the O (an optical correction: an open shape reads
      narrower than a closed one at equal width).
    * O, C and the digit 1 did not follow the width axis at all -- O and C
      because the bowl radius was bound as a Python default argument (evaluated
      once at import), the 1 because its base and flag were absolute.
- Version unified at 2.007 across all four masters.

1.000  (2026-07)
- Initial public release. One style (Regular), 172 glyphs.
- Design: geometric, strictly monolinear — the perpendicular stroke stays at
  90 units everywhere (enforced by the tools/qa_monoline.py gate, band
  88.5-91.9 u). Flat-topped / flat-bottomed hexagonal "alveole" cells,
  vertical side edges, bevelled and rounded corners.
- Coverage (French / Western-European Latin): a-z A-Z 0-9, ASCII punctuation
  and symbols, guillemets « » ‹ ›, ligatures œ æ Œ Æ, the full set of French
  accents (à â ä é è ê ë î ï ô ö ù û ü ÿ ç and capitals), math signs
  (+ − = < >), and € © ® § ¢ £ ¥ ° • … among others.
- Outlines: single-story monoline construction; round glyphs and pointed
  apices carry optical overshoots so they do not read smaller than the flat
  glyphs. Accented letters are TrueType composites (base + shared mark).
- Features: class-based GPOS kerning (features/kern.fea), GSUB ccmp NFD->NFC
  normalisation + GDEF, and a gasp table. Hinted with ttfautohint.
- Vertical metrics follow the Google Fonts recipe (USE_TYPO_METRICS armed) so
  accented capitals do not clip on macOS / Safari.
- Scope is deliberately French / Western-European Latin: the full Google Fonts
  Latin Core is not targeted (the fontbakery glyph_coverage check is
  allow-listed in tools/ci_gate.py, not a build regression).
- Licensed under the SIL Open Font License 1.1, Reserved Font Name
  "Beeraw Hex".
